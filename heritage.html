Heritage

Heritage Zones

Step into the living history of George Town, Penang — a city where centuries-old streets, vibrant cultures, and timeless architecture come together in a breathtaking tapestry of East and West. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2008, George Town’s heritage area is lovingly preserved within two distinctive zones: the Core Zone and the Buffer Zone.

Street Art

In Penang, art comes alive on the streets. Colorful murals and playful sculptures transform alleyways and heritage walls into an open-air gallery. Born from the city’s rich multicultural history, Penang’s street art blends local stories, traditional trades, and nostalgic childhood scenes with a touch of humor and whimsy.

Places of Worship

Penang’s diverse heritage shines through its historic places of worship, where mosques, temples, and churches stand side by side. Highlights include the 19th-century Masjid Kapitan Keling, Chinese clan temples, Hindu shrines, and colonial churches — each a reflection of the city’s rich, multicultural past and spirit of harmony.
